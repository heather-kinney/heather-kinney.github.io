Current Projects

Fish Passage Monitoring – PIT Tagging on the Saugatucket River

Using Passive Integrated Transponder (PIT) telemetry to track river herring migration, assessing fish passage effectiveness and population movement through restored habitats.

Artificial Reef Installation – Sabin Point Park

Deployment and monitoring of artificial reefs to enhance marine habitat complexity and support biodiversity, with a focus on fish and invertebrate communities.

Fish Habitat Enhancement – Providence River Estuary

Monitoring the impact of habitat restoration efforts on fish populations and ecosystem health in urban estuarine environments.

Cyanobacteria Monitoring – Roger Williams Park & Freshwater Bodies

Tracking harmful algal blooms (HABs) to understand trends, inform public health advisories, and improve freshwater ecosystem management.

Experimental Oyster Reefs – Coastal Ponds

Evaluating the success of oyster reef restoration in enhancing water quality, increasing biodiversity, and providing coastal resilience.

Brood Stock Enhancement – Narrow River

Surveying and analyzing the health of fish stocks to guide conservation strategies and fisheries management.

Wild Oyster Stock Monitoring – Shellfish Restoration Enhancement Plan

Assessing natural oyster populations to support restoration planning and sustainable management efforts.

Juvenile Fish Seine Survey – Upper Bay

Conducting seine surveys from May to October at 12 stations to monitor fish populations and habitat changes. This long-term dataset (2017–2024) helps track trends and ecosystem shifts.
