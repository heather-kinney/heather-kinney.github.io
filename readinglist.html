A Different Kind of Resource List

    Science isn’t just something we do in the field—it’s something we engage with, challenge, and expand through reading. This list includes books that have shaped my understanding of coastal and marine science, informed my work, or simply inspired me to think differently about the natural world. Whether you’re a fellow scientist, a student, or just someone curious about our coasts and oceans, I hope you find something here that resonates.

Books

The Ocean of Life

by Callum Roberts

A captivating look into the history and future of our oceans, blending science and storytelling to reveal the fragile beauty of marine life.

Blue Mind

by Wallace J. Nichols

Nichols explores the deep connection between water and our well-being, inspiring a renewed appreciation for our planet’s blue spaces.

Running Silver: Restoring Atlantic Rivers and Their Great Fish Migrations

Author: John Waldman

This book is an essential read for understanding the historical and ecological importance of migratory fish species like river herring, shad, and Atlantic salmon. Waldman dives into the impact of human development on these species and presents solutions for restoring fish passage and river ecosystems.

Life Between the Tides

Author: Adam Nicolson

A beautifully written exploration of the intertidal zone, blending science, philosophy, and personal reflection. Nicolson captures the dynamic life found in the space between land and sea, offering a fresh perspective on marine ecosystems and our connection to them.
